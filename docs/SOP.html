1. What the tool is

The NTM Quoting Tool is the self-service web app at the quoting portal where prospective customers price an NTM managed-IT package, sign a contract, and pay their first month — all in one sitting. When a customer pays, the tool automatically provisions the company in ConnectWise (creates the Company, Contact, Opportunity, Agreement with line items, and an onboarding Project), emails the signed contract PDF, and notifies sales.

You — the admin — control everything the customer sees and how the back-end behaves, from the /admin portal. You never have to edit code to change a price, a package, a discount, a piece of wording, or the AI assistant's behavior. This document is the playbook for both sides.

Customer Workflow
What a customer sees from the moment they land on the tool to the moment they pay.

2. The customer path, step by step

Step 1 — Choose a package

The customer lands on the package picker. They see only the packages you've made customer-visible (e.g. SafeSecure, SafeSecure Plus). Each card shows the headline per-user price and per-location price, the contract term length (month-to-month, 3-year, or 5-year), and the bundled features.

They click the package they want and hit Build a Quote.

Step 2 — Service details & contact info

On the next screen they tell us the size of their team and how to reach them:

  • Desktop Users — staff who need full Microsoft 365 Business Premium. At least one is required.
  • Web Users — frontline / kiosk / shared-device people who only need email and browser apps (Microsoft 365 F3). Optional.
  • Number of Locations — physical sites we'll manage.
  • Contact info — name, business name, email, phone, address.
  • Optional add-ons — VoIP, Microsoft Teams Phone, eFaxing, Microsoft SaaS Backups, Server Management per VM.

Step 3 — Summary

The customer reviews their full quote: package, per-tier user counts, add-ons, the upfront onboarding fee (waived for online portal customers), the first-month charge, and the ongoing monthly recurring amount. If they have a promo code, they apply it here.

Step 4 — Terms & review

The Terms page shows the full Managed Services Agreement they're agreeing to. The Review page is the final stop before paying: it shows the same numbers plus a Preview full Quote & Contract button that opens the complete two-part document (the QUOTE section + the CONTRACT section) in a new tab.

Step 5 — E-sign and pay

They type their name to e-sign, tick the agreement checkbox, and hit Pay [amount] & Start Services. They're redirected to Alternative Payments' secure hosted checkout to complete payment by card or ACH.

Step 6 — Done

On successful payment, the customer sees the Payment Success page and receives one email titled "Your Contract — [Business Name]" with the signed PDF attached. The PDF has two clearly labeled parts:

  • Part 1 — QUOTE: pricing breakdown, add-ons, financial summary, admin notes (if any).
  • Part 2 — CONTRACT: legal parties, agreement details, terms & conditions, signature block (pre-signed by NTM, signed by the customer).

Recurring invoices ship from CW on the 1st of every month and are Net 30.

The AI chat assistant

On the package picker, a floating chat button appears in the bottom-right. The first time a customer visits, a small popover offers help. The assistant can:

  • Answer questions about packages, prices, and add-ons.
  • Recommend a package based on what the customer describes.
  • Pre-fill form fields with values the customer gives in chat.
  • If a question is outside its knowledge, offer to schedule a follow-up call with a sales rep (opens the GHL booking widget).

Returning customers — quote lookup

Below the package cards, customers who already have a quote can click "Have a quote already? Look it up" and either paste their quote number (QT-...) or their email to find every quote tied to them.

4. The day-to-day: managing quotes

Finding a quote

Open Quotes. Each row shows quote number, customer, package, totals, status (draft / sent / accepted / checkout_pending / paid), and CW provisioning state. Filter by status and search by customer name, email, or quote number.

The quote detail page

Click any row to open it. At the top you'll see status badges and a row of action buttons:

  • Customer View — opens the quote as the customer sees it.
  • Contract Preview — renders the contract PDF preview.
  • Edit Quote — opens the inline editor for package / sizing / add-ons / pricing / notes.
  • Retry CW (shows only when a step failed) — re-runs the ConnectWise provisioning from wherever it stopped.
  • Delete Quote — hard-deletes the quote row plus its generated contracts. Existing CW records are untouched.

Editing a quote

Click Edit Quote. You'll get a panel where you can:

  • Change the selected package (admin-only packages like Essentials appear here even when hidden from customers).
  • Change the contract term (MTM / 3-year / 5-year).
  • Adjust Desktop Users, Web Users, and Locations counts.
  • Override the per-Desktop / per-Web / per-Location prices for this one quote — useful for one-off discounts or custom scoping. The canonical package row stays untouched.
  • Toggle individual add-ons on/off and set quantities.
  • Add free-text Notes the customer will see on their review page and in the contract PDF.
Editing a paid quote
If the quote has already been paid, saving the edit doesn't overwrite it — instead, the system creates an amendment quote (a new row linked to the original) and a fresh AP invoice for the price delta. The original record stays intact as the audit trail.

Applying an admin-only discount (e.g. the 5-year discount)

On the quote detail page, scroll to "Admin-only discounts". Active admin-only promo codes are listed there with badges showing the discount and which line they apply to. Click Apply to attach the promo to the quote — totals recalc immediately and the discount also appears as a negative-priced line on the ConnectWise agreement when provisioning runs (provided you've set the CW Product ID on the promo).

Custom line items

The Custom Line Items section at the bottom lets you add one-off charges (recurring or one-time) that aren't in the standard catalog. Use this for custom integration scope, special hardware, etc.

Updating the Tool
How to change prices, packages, copy, AI behavior, and discounts without touching code.

5. Updating packages

Go to Packages. Each package has its own card.

Pricing

Three per-unit prices:

  • Desktop User — Microsoft 365 Business Premium tier (primary staff).
  • Web User — F3 / Web & Email Only tier (frontline / kiosk).
  • Per Location — per physical site.

Contract Term

Pick Month-to-month, 3-year, or 5-year from the dropdown. Customers see the label on the package card. Changing it here updates new quotes immediately; quotes already in flight keep the term they were signed under.

"Show on customer page" toggle

Off = the package is hidden from the customer picker but still usable from the admin Edit Quote panel. This is how Essentials is currently configured.

CW IDs

Each package needs four ConnectWise IDs filled in so provisioning works:

  • CW Agreement Type ID
  • Per-User Product ID (Business Premium)
  • F3 Per-User Product ID (Web & Email Only)
  • Per-Location Product ID

These come from ConnectWise's procurement catalog. The seed file already has the production-correct IDs for the three canonical packages, but if you add a new package or want to swap a SKU, paste the numeric ID here.

Click Save All Packages at the bottom when you're done. Changes are live immediately.

6. Updating add-ons

Go to Add-Ons. Each add-on has a name, description, recurring price, pricing type (recurring-only / one-time-only / both), an active toggle, and a CW Product ID.

The CW Product ID is the bookkeeping link. Even if the catalog's default price differs from what you're charging the customer, the tool will post the customer's price to CW so the invoice matches the quote.

7. Updating promo codes

Go to Promo Codes. For each code you set:

  • Code — what the customer types (or what admin applies internally for admin-only).
  • Discount amount + discount type (percentage / fixed).
  • Apply to — one-time / monthly / onboarding.
  • Active toggle.
  • Admin-only toggle — when on, the code is hidden from the customer wizard. Only applicable from the quote detail page.
  • CW Product ID (optional) — when set, the discount also gets posted as a negative-priced line on the ConnectWise agreement so CW invoices match.
5-Year Discount setup
The 5YR-DISCOUNT promo ships pre-seeded as admin-only at 15% off monthly. Set its CW Product ID to PERUSER0004-MRR's numeric id from CW for the discount to flow through to ConnectWise invoices. Without it, the discount applies only to the quote total + the AP first-month invoice; CW will continue to invoice the full monthly amount.

8. Updating customer-facing copy

Go to Site Copy. You can edit:

  • Page heading — the big title above the package cards.
  • Subheading — one or two sentences underneath.
  • Explainer popover title — the title shown in the (i) icon popover on the Service Details page.
  • Explainer body — the multi-paragraph Desktop-vs-Web User explanation. Blank line = paragraph break.

Changes are live as soon as you click Save Site Copy.

9. Updating Terms & Conditions

Go to Terms. The content here is what appears in Part 2 — Contract of every PDF. Versioned: when you update, the previous version is kept on file so historical quotes keep showing the terms they were signed under.

10. Configuring the AI Chat assistant

Turning it on

Open AI Chat. In the top-right of the page there's a big Live for customers / Disabled toggle — flip it on and it persists immediately. The OpenRouter API key must be set in Integrations first; if it's missing you'll see a banner with a link to fix it.

Editing the assistant's behavior

Open the Prompt + Tools tab. The "Playbook prompt" textarea controls the assistant's persona, conversational tone, and step-by-step flow. Edit freely — what you write here is what the customer's chat session uses.

A small banner at the top of that tab reminds you: guardrails (security and correctness rules) live in code and are always prepended automatically. You can't disable them from here, and you don't need to repeat them in your playbook — the model already has them. Things like "never invent prices, always quote from the live page snapshot, never agree to terms for the customer" are handled.

Knowledge base

Open the Knowledge base tab. Two ways to add docs:

  • Upload file — drop a PDF, DOCX, TXT, MD, or CSV. The tool extracts the text and stores it as a KB doc you can review or edit.
  • New doc — paste markdown directly.

Active KB docs are concatenated into every chat turn so the agent can answer specific NTM questions (policies, FAQs, service descriptions) without making things up.

Model, cost caps, rate limits

The General tab lets you switch primary/fallback models, adjust temperature, set per-session and daily USD spend caps, and rate-limit how fast a session can fire messages. Sensible defaults are pre-set.

11. ConnectWise integration setup

Credentials

Go to Integrations. CW credentials live here: company id, public key, private key, client id, base URL. You can edit them in the admin portal — no redeploy needed.

Reference IDs

Go to CW IDs. The tool needs CW-side numeric IDs for things like opportunity status, project board, project template, currency, etc. Default values are pre-filled and audited against NTM production. Open the page to confirm or adjust.

Project template auto-discovery

At the top of the CW IDs page there's a Project template auto-discovery card. It looks up the CW project template by name (default: "SafeSecure Pure SaaS (Project Template) v3") and writes the id back to the config. Click Find and set template id once after deploy — every new customer onboarding project will then use that template.

Part 4 of 4
When Things Go Wrong
Where to look and what to do when CW provisioning, email delivery, or payment hits a snag.

12. The Logs tab

Every time a ConnectWise provisioning step fails, the tool:

  1. Records the failure to the database (visible on the quote detail page's step list).
  2. Writes a structured row to the Logs tab.
  3. Emails support@trustntm.com from logs@trustntm.com with the quote number, business name, step name, full error, and the CW IDs known so far.

Open the Logs tab to see every failure across all quotes. Click Details on a row to expand the CW context. Click Open quote to jump to the affected record and hit Retry CW.

Once you've worked through a batch of issues, hit Clear all to wipe the logs. (Doing this doesn't touch the affected quotes or any CW records — it only deletes the audit log rows.)

13. Common scenarios

"A customer paid but nothing showed up in CW"

  1. Open the quote on the Quotes tab.
  2. Look at the step list. If a step is failed/partial, the error message is right there.
  3. Fix the root cause — usually a missing CW reference ID or product ID. Set it on the appropriate admin page.
  4. Click Retry CW at the top. The orchestrator resumes from the failed step; successful earlier steps short-circuit.

"The customer needs a different price than the standard package"

  1. Open the quote on the Quotes tab.
  2. Click Edit Quote.
  3. Adjust the price-override fields ($/Desktop User, $/Web User, $/Location) for this one quote.
  4. Click Save Changes. The canonical package stays the same; only this quote uses the new numbers.

"The customer wants to add a service after they've paid"

  1. Open the paid quote.
  2. Click Edit Quote, toggle on the new add-on, set quantity, save.
  3. The system creates an amendment quote (a new row linked to the original) and a fresh AP invoice for the additional cost. You'll be redirected to the amendment.
  4. Forward the amendment's payment link to the customer.

"The contract PDF is wrong / has stale wording"

Two places to check: Terms (the legal terms text in Part 2) and Site Copy (the customer-facing wording on the picker / Service Details — does not appear in the contract). Edit, save, and re-preview from the quote detail page's Contract Preview button.

"The AI chat is saying weird things / making things up"

  1. Open AI Chat → Prompt + Tools.
  2. Adjust the playbook prompt to clarify the behavior you want. The system prompt is just text — describe it like you'd describe it to a new sales rep.
  3. Click Save all settings.
  4. If the issue is missing factual info, open the Knowledge base tab and upload or write a KB doc covering it. KB docs are concatenated into every chat turn.

"I need to look at the contract before sending it"

Two paths:

  • From Quote Detail → Contract Preview — renders the same template the signed PDF uses.
  • From the customer side: the Preview full Quote & Contract button on the Review page does the same thing.

Either way, the customer's signature line is blank in the preview, and the service-provider side shows the pre-signed countersignature.

Where to start when in doubt
  1. For a specific quote issue: Quotes → click row → look at the step list / errors.
  2. For a pattern of issues across many quotes: Logs tab.
  3. For pricing or product config: Packages / Add-Ons / Promo Codes.
  4. For customer-facing wording: Site Copy (picker text) or Terms (legal terms in the contract).
  5. For the AI assistant: AI Chat → Prompt + Tools / Knowledge base.
